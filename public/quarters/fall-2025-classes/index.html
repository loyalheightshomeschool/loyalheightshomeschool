Anthropology of Fibers and Textiles

Instructor: Karen Radcliff
Ages: 12 - 18
Maximum class size: 8
$175
In this class, we’ll explore the fascinating intersection of fiber arts and anthropology. We will talk about the roles of fiber and cloth from an anthropological perspective while working on a small sample activity each week. Our discussions will range from the importance of string to fishing and animal domestication, to the types of clothing that developed in different areas, to the role of cloth in ritual and religion. One week, we may discuss fabric re-use and how it has developed (out of a necessity) into different art forms, while using strips of cloth from old textiles to weaving coasters (using the Japanese sakiori weaving technique). Another week, we may try our hands at sprang, a netting technique, while discussing its use in the American Revolutionary War. Perhaps we’ll talk about what makes a cloth appropriate to ritual, and design one ourselves. No prior experience is necessary. The instructor will provide all the needed patterns and materials.

Anthropology through Food

Instructor: Karen Radcliff
Ages: 12 - 18
Maximum class size: 10
$175
Food. We all eat it, but not in the same way. Nor do we prepare it the same, find the same things delicious, obtain foods the same way, or have the same historical relationships to food. It’s a human universal to eat, and one of humanity’s strengths is our creative omnivorism. In this course we will explore the evolution of eating, the changing material culture of food, the relationship of culture to food, and how the necessity of eating has affected diverse aspects of human culture.

Instructor: Stephanie Burkemoore
Ages: 12 - 18
Maximum class size: 10
